\# AI Ethics Statement



I believe AI ethics should go beyond just stating general ideas like fairness or transparency and actually focus on what people will do in practice. After reading Chapter 5 of \*Ethical Machines\*, it’s clear that ethics statements only matter if they lead to real actions. Due to  that, I think developers and organizations should clearly define who is responsible for AI outcomes, regularly check systems for bias, and be open about how their models are built and used.



In real-world situations, this means taking specific steps like reviewing data for bias, explaining limitations of AI systems to users, and making sure there is human oversight when decisions have serious consequences. Ethical AI shouldn’t just be about avoiding harm—it should also build trust and accountability. Overall, I think the most important part of AI ethics is making sure it actually changes how systems are designed and used, rather than just sounding good on paper.
